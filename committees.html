Committees:


JCC - Joint Crisis Committee

The Joint Crisis Committee (JCC) is a type of Model United Nations (MUN) committee where delegates represent multiple countries or organizations in a crisis situation. In a JCC, participants must work together to address a rapidly developing global or regional crisis, such as a natural disaster, conflict, or political emergency. Unlike traditional MUN committees, which follow a structured debate format, the JCC focuses on fast-paced decision-making and problem-solving, often involving real-time updates and unexpected twists. Delegates are expected to think on their feet, adapt to changing situations, and collaborate with others to form solutions.


UNWOMEN - United Nations Entity for Gender Equality and the Empowerment of Women

UN Women is the United Nations entity focused on promoting gender equality and empowering women. Established in 2010, it works globally to tackle issues like gender-based violence, economic inequality, and women's political participation. The organization supports policies and programs that advance women's rights and monitors progress toward gender equality goals, including those in the Sustainable Development Goals.


HUNSC - Historical United Nations Security Council

The Historical United Nations Security Council (HUNSC) is a type of Model United Nations (MUN) committee where delegates simulate the Security Council's actions during a specific historical event. Unlike the current Security Council, HUNSC focuses on past crises or conflicts, requiring delegates to represent countries as they were at that time, considering the political context and historical realities. This format challenges participants to understand and debate decisions based on the history and perspectives of that era.

Legal - United Nations Sixth Committee (Legal Committee)

The United Nations Sixth Committee, also known as the Legal Committee, is a primary forum for discussing international legal issues within the UN. It addresses topics like the development of international law, the codification of treaties, and the promotion of justice globally. The committee discusses matters such as human rights, the peaceful settlement of disputes, and the laws of war, working to ensure that legal frameworks are upheld in international relations. In MUN, delegates in the Legal Committee debate and draft resolutions on legal challenges facing the world.


ECOSOC - United Nations Economic and Social Council

The United Nations Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC) is one of the six main organs of the UN, responsible for coordinating economic, social, and related work across the UN system. It focuses on promoting global development, addressing issues like poverty, education, health, and human rights. ECOSOC oversees numerous specialized agencies and programs, helping to create policies and strategies to improve the well-being of people worldwide. In MUN, delegates in ECOSOC work on drafting resolutions to tackle global social and economic challenges.


UNICEF - United Nations International Children’s Emergency Fund

UNICEF (United Nations International Children’s Emergency Fund) is a UN agency that works to protect and improve the lives of children worldwide. Founded in 1946, it focuses on issues like child health, education, nutrition, and protection from violence and exploitation. UNICEF provides emergency assistance in crises and advocates for children's rights, aiming to ensure that every child has the opportunity to grow up healthy, educated, and safe.


IOC - International Olympic Committee

The International Olympic Committee (IOC) is the governing body responsible for organizing the Olympic Games and promoting the Olympic values worldwide. Founded in 1894, the IOC oversees the planning and execution of both the Summer and Winter Games, ensuring they align with the principles of sport, excellence, and fair competition. It also works to promote the role of sport in society and supports the development of athletics globally through various programs and initiatives.
